Changunarayan Temple

Changu Narayan is a temple located in the Kathmandu Valley of Nepal, dedicated to Lord Vishnu. It is considered to be one of the oldest temples in Nepal and is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The temple is located on a hilltop in the village of Changu, which is about 7 miles east of the city of Kathmandu. According to legend, the temple was built in the 4th century AD by King Hari Datta Varma. The temple has undergone many renovations and restorations over the years, with the most recent one in 2011 after it was damaged in an earthquake

Changu Narayan Temple is famous for its exquisite Newari architecture, intricate carvings, and beautiful stone sculptures. The temple has four main doors, which are guarded by life-sized statues of lions and other mythical creatures. The main courtyard of the temple is home to a beautiful stone statue of Garuda, a mythical bird and mount of Lord Vishnu.

Inside the temple, visitors can see several beautiful idols and sculptures of various Hindu deities. The temple is also home to a museum that displays various artifacts and exhibits related to the history and culture of Nepal.

One of the highlights of visiting Changu Narayan Temple is the spectacular view of the surrounding hills and countryside. Visitors can also take a leisurely stroll through the nearby village, which is famous for its traditional Newari architecture and handicrafts.

Overall, Changu Narayan Temple is an important religious and cultural site in Nepal, and a must-visit destination for those interested in ancient history and architecture.
