Kathmandu Darbaur Square

Kathmandu Durbar Square is a historic square located in the heart of the city of Kathmandu, Nepal. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its rich cultural and historical significance.

The square was originally built in the 12th century during the Malla dynasty, but much of the architecture that exists today was constructed in the 17th and 18th centuries under the rule of the Shah dynasty. The square was the royal palace of the Nepali monarchs and served as the administrative center of the city for many centuries. Kathmandu Durbar Square is home to several magnificent palaces, temples, and courtyards, each withits own unique architectural style. The Hanuman Dhoka Palace is the most significant building inthe square, which was once the residence of the Nepali monarchs. Other notable structures in thesquare include the Taleju Temple, the Jagannath Temple, and the Kumari Ghar, which is home to theLiving Goddess or Kumari of Kathmandu.

The square is also home to several beautiful stone carvings, statues, and other artistic creations. One of the most popular attractions is the intricately carved wooden peacock window at the Pujari Math temple.Kathmandu Durbar Square is also an important cultural center, and visitors can witness severalreligious festivals and ceremonies throughout the year. One of the most popular festivals is IndraJatra, which is held in September and involves a procession of chariots, masked dancers, andreligious performances.

Overall, Kathmandu Durbar Square is an important cultural and historical site in Nepal, and a must-visit destination for those interested in Nepalese architecture, history, and culture.
