Bhaktapur Darbaur Square

Bhaktapur Durbar Square is a historic square located in the ancient city of Bhaktapur, Nepal. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its rich cultural and historical significance.

The square was built during the 12th century during the reign of the Malla dynasty, and much of the architecture that exists today was constructed in the 15th and 17th centuries. Bhaktapur Durbar Square was the royal palace of the Bhaktapur Kingdom and served as the administrative center of the city for many centuries. Bhaktapur Durbar Square is home to several magnificent palaces, temples, and courtyards, each withits own unique architectural style. The Palace of Fifty-Five Windows is the most significantbuilding in the square, which was once the residence of the Bhaktapur monarchs. Other notablestructures in the square include the Nyatapola Temple, the Bhairavnath Temple, and the GoldenGate, which is adorned with beautiful carvings and statues.

The square is also home to several beautiful stone carvings, statues, and other artistic creations. One of the most popular attractions is the intricately carved wooden peacock window at the Pujari Math temple. Bhaktapur Durbar Square is also an important cultural center, and visitors can witness severalreligious festivals and ceremonies throughout the year. One of the most popular festivals is theBisket Jatra, which is held in April and involves a procession of chariots, masked dancers, andreligious performances.

Overall, Bhaktapur Durbar Square is an important cultural and historical site in Nepal, and a must-visit destination for those interested in Nepalese architecture, history, and culture.
